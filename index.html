Hi there!

My name is Cherie. Yes, it's pronounced like 'Sherry'.
It's okay, Starbucks misspells it all the time too.

Born in Canada with a Chinese heritage, I've spent the majority of my life in Seattle, Washington. So naturally, I have developed a strong coffee addiction (that is completely under-control), an enthusiasm for gorgeous mountains and endless bodies of water, and a mediocre tolerance for rain.

I am currently a student at the University of Washington, where I am pursuing degrees in Computer Science and Informatics. As reflected in my college career, not only do I love software development itself, but I am particularly interested in the product's impact on society. A budding full-stack developer, I aim to build software that solves today's pressing problems, and use technology to simplify people's lives.

Two years ago, I found the perfect job that blended my two passions: helping others, and blabbing about how cool programming is. As a teaching assistant for the Introductory Programming series at UW, I have absolutely enjoyed watching previously non-technical students fall in love with computer science. I now hold a management-heavy role, where I interview, hire, and train new TAs each quarter, and ensure that our TAs are teaching at a high standard.

I am also a TUNE House Scholar, which is an incredible scholarship that provides housing, mentorship, and tech workshops for eight undergraduate women studying Computer Science at UW. This program has supported and empowered me to pursue the technology industry despite under-representation, and I am endlessly grateful for the opportunities it has given me (like meeting Melinda Gates!).

Because society jokes that millenials can't pull themselves away from their computers, I feel obligated to mention that I am a runner, a sunset-enthusiast, and a strong-willed dog person. I thrive in sunshine and quaint coffee shops, speak conversational Mandarin at about a 4th grade level, and am a die-hard Taylor Swift fan. I have a stubborn sweet-tooth and am always looking for new dessert shops to try, so if you have any recommendations, let me know.

It's so nice to meet you!
